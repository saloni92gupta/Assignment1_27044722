About Rotorua

The name Rotorua comes from Māori, the full name for the city and lake is Te Rotorua - nui - a - Kahumatamomoe. Roto means 'lake' and rua means 'two' or in this case 'second' – Rotorua thus meaning 'Second lake'.

Rotorua Climate

The Rotorua region enjoys a mild temperate climate. Rotorua is situated inland from the coast and is sheltered by high country to the south and east of the city, resulting in less wind than many other places in New Zealand.

Services

Lake View

There are different 20 Lakes in Rotorua. Rotorua lake is the center of attraction as it is in the middle of Rotorua. Another two lakes are very known which are blue lake and Green lake. As well as there is a middle point location from which we can see both the lakes.

Hot Pools

This is extra ordinery place to visit in Rotorua. Basically here you can fine hot lakes of sulphurs, because of that the natural hot water can be produced. That is used for relaxation place.

Thursday Night Market

On every Thursday there is a night street market open for evening 6 to 9 at EAT street, near to Rotorua lake. It is a kind of street shopping of vegetables, food, assasories etc. There is also a cultural/ musical events to enjoy.
